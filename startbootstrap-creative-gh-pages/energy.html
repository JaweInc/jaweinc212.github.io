Energy, Technology and Infrastructure Practice Group (ETIPG)

The Nigerian energy, oil and gas sectors form a critical part of the country’s economic system. Some of the country’s largest transactions and disputes arise out of these areas. In this respect, over the years, our lawyers have been involved in some of Nigeria’s most complex and notable matters in the oil and gas industry. Our experience includes advising clients in the acquisition and disposal of interests in oil and gas assets, gas sale and purchase transactions, representing clients in environmental degradation claims arising from the operations of upstream oil companies in Nigeria, and representing clients in disputes arising from the sale and purchase of oil and gas assets.

Our ETIPG is active in the power sector, where we provide clients with advisory services on regulatory matters, power purchase and sale agreements and also represent clients in complex litigations arising from power sector disputes. Lawyers in our ETIPG track legal developments in the ever growing Nigerian technology industry and render advisory services to clients on legal aspects of technological issues that arise from their operations, including legislative compliance matters, such as under the Cybercrimes (Prohibition, Prevention, ETC) Act.
